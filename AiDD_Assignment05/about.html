The Story Behind the Data

From 40 million chaotic records to career-defining clarity

My "aha moment" came at 2 AM on a Tuesday, staring at a spreadsheet with 127 different material numbers for the same product. That's when I realized: bad data doesn't just slow companies down—it paralyzes decision-making.

I'm Aneesh Yaramati, and that chaotic spreadsheet at Schlumberger launched my obsession with data engineering. Over two years, I consolidated 10+ legacy systems into a single source of truth—40 million records that teams could finally trust. But the real win wasn't the technology; it was watching procurement teams stop second-guessing every number and start making confident decisions.

Why Data Engineering?

Most people see data engineering as plumbing—necessary but unglamorous. I see it differently. Every ETL pipeline I build, every validation rule I write, removes friction from someone's day. When data "just works," analysts spend time discovering insights instead of debugging discrepancies. Product managers make decisions based on truth, not hunches.

At Schlumberger, I didn't just move data—I built systems that teams actually used. My validation frameworks caught errors before they became million-dollar mistakes. My archival workflows improved system reliability by 20%. But what I'm proudest of? The thank-you emails from analysts who said, "This is the first time I trust these numbers."

The Kelley Chapter

Now I'm at Kelley School of Business, and here's why: I'm good at building data systems, but I want to be great at building data systems that drive business value. There's a gap between "technically correct" and "strategically brilliant," and I'm here to close it.

I'm combining my enterprise engineering experience with modern AI tools, business strategy, and product thinking. The goal? To bridge the gap between data teams and business stakeholders—speaking both languages fluently.

Current Focus Areas

  • Cloud Architecture: Mastering Azure and AWS to build scalable, cost-effective data platforms
  • Modern Data Stack: Experimenting with dbt, Airflow, and Snowflake—moving beyond legacy tools
  • AI-Powered Development: Leveraging AI assistants while maintaining code quality and documentation standards
  • Open Source: Contributing to data quality frameworks because better tools lift everyone

And yes, I occasionally debug my own photography workflow scripts (old habits die hard).
